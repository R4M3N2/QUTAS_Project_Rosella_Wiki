Project Rosella is a competition focused on the design and construction of autonomous competition drones. Competitors will be split into teams of five to six people, the teams will elect a leader of the group who can help manage the overall direction of the team. The competition will last for a total of ten weeks, aligning with the semester timeline. 

Each week there will be a workshop hosted for both the major sections. These workshops will help competitors and teams learn about drone structures and to be able to use unit and class education practically.

The competition is split into two major sections with two minor sections;

Guidance, Navigation, and Control [WIP]

- Focused on the design and fabrication of guidance, navigation, and control components of the RC drone. This includes electrical work. 
- Examples of this section include connecting the drone to ground control, connecting electrical components together.

Aerostructures [WIP]

- Focused on the design and fabrication of the structural and aerodynamic design of the drone. 
- Examples of this section include CAD modelling of the drone, making sure the wings provide lift.

Business [WIP]

- The business section focuses on the work related to the management of V-Bucks and business proposals inside the competition.

Creative Arts [WIP]

- The creative arts section does art related things. 
- Examples include the design of livery designs, and simple 3D animations of the team’s drone.

The competition will use a competition currency called Vehicle Bucks (V-Bucks for short), which can be used to increase the weight allowance of the drone and unlock additional and better performing parts.
